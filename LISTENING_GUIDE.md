Three compositions generated by `test_compositions.py` to push the pipeline
beyond its default "C minor sonata" comfort zone.

## 1. Sonata Exposition in D minor (String Quartet)

**File:** `test_sonata_d_minor.mid`
**Duration:** ~87 seconds | **Tempo:** 132 BPM | **Bars:** 48

### What to listen for

- **P-theme (bars 1-14):** The first violin states the primary theme in D minor
  with a heroic, driving character. Listen for the characteristic galant schemata
  (Do-Re-Mi, Jupiter, Cudworth) that give the theme its Classical-era shape.
  The other strings provide SATB harmonic support beneath.

- **Transition (bars 15-24):** The texture shifts to polyphonic writing -- all
  four voices become more independent and agitated. The harmony destabilizes,
  preparing the modulation to the relative major (F major). Listen for the Monte
  and Ponte schemata creating rising sequential energy.

- **S-theme (bars 25-38):** A lyrical contrast in F major. The first violin
  sings a more song-like melody over gentler accompaniment. Prinner and Fenaroli
  schemata give this section its characteristic descending, settling quality.
  Ends with a strong Perfect Authentic Cadence in F major.

- **Closing theme (bars 39-48):** A playful, lighter texture in homophonic
  style that confirms the new key of F major. Shorter, more balanced phrases.

### Character arc
Heroic tension (D minor) -> agitated energy -> lyrical release (F major) -> playful confirmation.

## 2. Theme and 4 Variations in F major (Piano)

**File:** `test_variations_f_major.mid`
**Duration:** ~256 seconds (4:16) | **Tempo:** 60 BPM | **Bars:** 64

### What to listen for

- **Theme (bars 1-12):** A serene melody in F major with Alberti bass
  accompaniment in the left hand. The slow tempo (60 BPM) gives this a
  calm, contemplative quality. The galant schemata provide a simple,
  elegant harmonic framework.

- **Variation 1 (bars 13-25):** Lyrical character with Alberti bass texture.
  The theme's harmonic skeleton remains, but the surface rhythm and
  ornamentation change. Listen for how the melody decorates the original
  structural tones.

- **Variation 2 (bars 26-38):** Shifts to agitated character with polyphonic
  texture. The mood darkens and the voices become more contrapuntal.
  This is the dramatic contrast variation.

- **Variation 3 (bars 39-51):** Mysterious character, homophonic texture.
  Softer dynamics (pp), portato articulation. Listen for the atmospheric
  change -- the same harmonies feel entirely different in this setting.

- **Variation 4 (bars 52-64):** Triumphant character with arpeggio texture.
  The final variation brings energy and resolution, with louder dynamics
  and marcato articulation closing the piece decisively.

### Character arc
Serene -> lyrical elaboration -> agitated contrast -> mysterious atmosphere -> triumphant conclusion.

## 3. Ternary Form in Bb minor (Orchestra)

**File:** `test_ternary_bb_minor.mid`
**Duration:** ~131 seconds (2:11) | **Tempo:** 66 BPM | **Bars:** 36

### What to listen for

- **A section (bars 1-13):** The strings (violin 1 with melody, violin 2,
  viola, cello for harmony) establish the mysterious Bb minor atmosphere.
  Wind doublings (flute, oboe, clarinet, bassoon, horn) add orchestral
  color and depth. Soft dynamics (pp) with portato articulation create a
  hushed, enigmatic texture. The slow tempo and flat-key tonality give
  this an especially dark, veiled quality.

- **B section (bars 14-23):** Modulates to Db major (relative major).
  The character shifts to lyrical -- a warmer, more open sound despite
  the continued soft dynamics. The Prinner and Pastorella schemata
  provide a gently descending, pastoral quality. Listen for how the
  orchestral texture opens up slightly in the major key.

- **A' section (bars 24-36):** Return to Bb minor and the mysterious
  character. The original material returns, but with subtle variation from
  the random schema selection. The piece ends in the home key with a
  Perfect Authentic Cadence.

### Character arc
Mysterious darkness (Bb minor) -> lyrical warmth (Db major) -> return to mystery.

### Orchestration notes
All 10 instruments are active:
- **Melody:** Violin 1 (with flute and oboe doublings)
- **Inner voices:** Violin 2 (alto), Viola (tenor) with clarinet and horn doublings
- **Bass:** Cello with bass and bassoon doublings

## Known Limitations

These compositions expose several areas where the pipeline could improve:

1. **Melodic stepwise ratio:** The variations piece scores 0.29 on stepwise
   motion (target is 0.45+), indicating too many leaps in the melody.

2. **Schema repetition:** Within each section, the same schemata repeat
   to fill bar counts, creating pattern-level repetition. A future
   improvement could vary the realization of repeated schemata.

3. **Variation differentiation:** While each variation uses a different
   character and texture token, the underlying harmonic skeleton is
   identical. True Classical variations would introduce mode changes,
   rhythmic augmentation, and more radical transformations.

4. **Orchestral independence:** Wind doublings currently shadow the string
   voices exactly. Real orchestral writing would feature independent wind
   melodies, tutti/solo contrast, and antiphonal textures.
